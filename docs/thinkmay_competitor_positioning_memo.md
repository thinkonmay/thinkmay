# Thinkmay Competitor & Positioning Memo

_Date: 2026-05-21_

## Purpose

This memo summarizes Thinkmay’s position in the Vietnam cloud gaming / cloud PC market using:

- internal product and marketing docs;
- existing Thinkmay SEO and brand research in `docs/`;
- fresh DFS-MCP keyword, SERP, and competitor data for Vietnam.

The goal is to answer three questions:

1. How healthy is the market?
2. Where does Thinkmay currently sit in that market?
3. What positioning should Thinkmay lean into next?

## Executive summary

Thinkmay is in a healthy but still relatively early category in Vietnam.

The market signal is real:

- `cloud gaming` has about **3,600 searches/month** in Vietnam;
- `cloud game` also has about **3,600/month**;
- `cloud pc` has about **2,400/month** and has shown recent growth;
- competition and CPC remain relatively low, which suggests the category is still under-owned.

Thinkmay already has a strong foothold in the more product-specific part of the market:

- strong visibility for **Cloud PC** terms;
- weaker visibility for the broad head term **cloud gaming**;
- a product that is more flexible and locally resonant than catalog-only global cloud gaming brands.

The strategic implication is simple:

- use **cloud gaming** to capture attention and search demand;
- use **Cloud PC** to explain the actual product model;
- use **quán net trên mây** as the emotionally memorable local brand frame.

Thinkmay should not position itself as “just another cloud gaming service.”
Its strongest identity is:

> **A mobile-first personal Cloud PC for Vietnamese gamers who want internet-cafe-grade gaming on weak devices, with saved data, installed games, and local-market convenience.**

## Product truth: what Thinkmay actually is

Based on the product and technical docs, Thinkmay is more than a streaming layer.

Core realities:

- a **persistent Windows Cloud PC** with saved data and installable apps/games;
- built for **gamers first**, but also usable for 3D/design workloads;
- optimized for **browser-based low-latency access** across PC, laptop, Mac, iOS, and Android;
- heavily shaped by a **mobile-first user base**;
- strongest current use cases include **AAA gaming on weak devices** and **modded/social titles like GTA V FiveM**.

This matters because the product is not equivalent to:

- Xbox Cloud Gaming / GeForce NOW style catalog access only;
- generic remote desktop software;
- pure GPU rental / infrastructure products.

Thinkmay sits between those categories, but it is closest to:

> **Cloud gaming for discovery, Cloud PC in the actual experience.**

### Trend read

- `cloud gaming` demand in Vietnam is steady and real, with recent monthly peaks around **4,400**.
- `cloud pc` appears to be growing from a smaller base, which is useful because it maps better to Thinkmay’s actual product.
- The category still looks underbuilt from an SEO and brand-ownership perspective.

### Market health conclusion

The market is not fully mainstream, but it is healthy enough to support a focused category leader.

This is a good environment for Thinkmay because:

- the search terms are already understood by some users;
- the category is not yet locked up by a single dominant local brand;
- there is room for a local company with stronger product-market fit than generic global messaging.

## Current SERP reality in Vietnam

For the broad `cloud gaming` query in Vietnam, Thinkmay is in the market but does not yet own the category.

Fresh DFS findings indicate:

- Thinkmay is around **#2 for `cloud pc`**;
- Thinkmay is around **#1 for `cloud gaming pc`**;
- Thinkmay is around **#5–6 for `cloud game`**;
- Thinkmay is only around **#14–19 for `cloud gaming`** depending dataset snapshot.

The live `cloud gaming` SERP is crowded by:

- **Xbox**;
- **CloudMoon**;
- **app-store pages**;
- **Thegioididong** and other explainer/editorial pages;
- **local telecom/media properties** such as MobiGames.

This means:

1. the broad term is competitive in a messy way;
2. some winners are not direct product substitutes, but SERP competitors;
3. Thinkmay is stronger when the query becomes more product-intent-specific.

## Competitor map

### 1. Global catalog cloud gaming brands

Examples:

- Xbox Cloud Gaming
- GeForce NOW

What they represent:

- instant recognition of the cloud gaming category;
- large brand trust;
- content/catalog-first mental model.

Where they are strong:

- head-term awareness;
- app-store and international brand presence;
- simple promise: play supported games anywhere.

Where Thinkmay can win:

- real personal machine feel, not just catalog access;
- local community and local support;
- better fit for modded, persistent, and install-your-own-game usage.

### 2. App-first cloud gaming platforms

Example:

- CloudMoon

What they represent:

- aggressive mobile-facing cloud gaming language;
- app-store/social proof visibility;
- a simpler mainstream framing than “Cloud PC.”

Risk to Thinkmay:

- they can absorb generic `cloud gaming` demand before users understand the deeper product difference.

Opportunity for Thinkmay:

- avoid competing only on the vague promise of “play games in the cloud.”
- compete on **ownership, persistence, flexibility, and local trust**.

### 3. Local cloud PC / GPU-rental adjacent competitors

Example:

- EZYCLOUDX

What they represent:

- direct overlap with Cloud PC intent;
- stronger comparability to Thinkmay’s actual product category.

Why they matter:

- they compete more directly for transactional searchers who already understand the Cloud PC model;
- they can outrank Thinkmay on some high-intent Cloud PC terms.

Thinkmay advantage:

- stronger gamer-native framing;
- clearer mobile-first use case;
- more emotionally resonant product story if branded correctly.

### 4. Publisher / explainer / aggregator pages

Examples:

- Thegioididong
- Viettel IDC articles
- app-store listing pages

What they represent:

- informational SERP gravity;
- strong domain authority;
- broad user education before product consideration.

Why they matter:

- they may beat product companies on the head term even if they do not sell the best service.

Thinkmay response:

- own more of the educational layer with dedicated landing pages and explainers;
- create pages that bridge “what is cloud gaming?” into “why Thinkmay is different.”

## Thinkmay’s actual position today

### Strengths

Live GSC validation on 2026-05-21 reinforces this positioning: Thinkmay remains strongest on Cloud PC / CloudPC-style terms, shows meaningful traction for rental-intent variants such as `thuê cloud pc`, and already has a standout organic foothold in Indonesia through `https://thinkmay.net/id/`.

1. **Strong fit with real Vietnam behavior**
   - mobile-first users;
   - weak laptop users;
   - internet-cafe replacement behavior;
   - social/modded game use cases.

2. **Clear product differentiation**
   - saved data;
   - persistent installs;
   - personal machine continuity;
   - browser-based access across devices.

3. **Better than average local emotional frame**
   - “quán net trên mây” is much more memorable than generic cloud-compute language.

4. **Existing category foothold**
   - especially strong on Cloud PC and mixed cloud gaming PC intent.

5. **Traction is real**
   - internal docs already frame Thinkmay as beyond pure validation stage, with meaningful paid usage and revenue.

### Weaknesses / risks

1. **Generic `cloud gaming` still under-owned**
   - bigger brands and stronger informational domains still intercept that term.

2. **Category confusion**
   - if Thinkmay says only “cloud gaming,” users may assume catalog-only access;
   - if Thinkmay says only “Cloud PC,” mainstream audiences may not instantly understand the benefit.

3. **Latency skepticism**
   - this is a permanent category tax;
   - users will doubt competitive play, mobile smoothness, and anti-cheat compatibility.

4. **Overpromising risk**
   - brand damage will come from broad claims like “works perfectly for everything” more than from being specific.

5. **Search dependence on branded / semi-branded strength**
   - Thinkmay already has branded power, but non-brand category capture still has room to improve.

## Positioning recommendation

Thinkmay should use a three-layer system.

### Layer 1: acquisition language

Use:

- **cloud gaming**
- **cloud game**
- **cloud gaming pc**

Purpose:

- capture search demand;
- fit social and ad hooks;
- match the language mainstream users already recognize.

### Layer 2: product explanation

Use:

- **Cloud PC**
- **Cloud PC riêng**
- **máy tính trên mây có thể lưu dữ liệu**

Purpose:

- explain what users are actually getting;
- separate Thinkmay from catalog-only cloud gaming competitors;
- justify retention, saved progress, and installed software benefits.

### Layer 3: emotional local brand

Use:

- **quán net trên mây**

Purpose:

- make the concept memorable;
- connect to an existing Vietnamese mental model;
- differentiate Thinkmay from cold, generic global cloud gaming language.

### Alternate version

Support line:

> **Không chỉ stream game. Bạn có một Cloud PC riêng để lưu save, cài game, giữ mod và quay lại bất cứ lúc nào.**

## What Thinkmay should emphasize against competitors

### Against Xbox / GeForce NOW

Emphasize:

- your own machine;
- save data and persistence;
- install your own games/apps;
- local-market accessibility and support.

### Against app-first cloud gaming brands

Emphasize:

- higher-trust serious product;
- not just “play a title,” but “keep your own setup”; 
- better for modded/social/long-session use cases.

### Against local Cloud PC competitors

Emphasize:

- gamer-native identity;
- mobile-first accessibility;
- emotional “internet cafe in the cloud” framing;
- better discovery and community loop.

## Messaging guardrails

Thinkmay should avoid:

- sounding like a generic cloud infrastructure vendor;
- sounding like a catalog-only cloud gaming app;
- promising perfect performance for all games and all networks;
- forcing users to understand deep technical architecture before they understand the benefit.

Thinkmay should lean into:

- weak device -> strong gaming outcome;
- phone/laptop-to-AAA surprise factor;
- saved progress and mod persistence;
- realistic latency expectations with proof;
- local community and local payments.

## Strategic conclusion

Thinkmay is well-positioned to become one of the strongest local category owners in Vietnam, but the company should not fight the market with only one label.

The winning structure is:

- **cloud gaming** for reach;
- **Cloud PC** for conversion and retention understanding;
- **quán net trên mây** for memory and brand distinction.

If Thinkmay executes this consistently, it can occupy a stronger position than “another cloud gaming service” and become:

> **the default mobile-first Cloud PC brand for weak-device gamers in Vietnam.**
